Vodka

The History of Vodka:Vodka is one of the oldest liquors ever produced, coming from the Russian word pronounced "voda", meaning water. It's been said that it's been around as early as the 8th or 9th century, but it's agreed that the first distillery was reported in the Vyatka Chronicle of 1174. Poland claims to have distilled vodka even earlier than the 8th century, but it wasn't like the vodka we know today. There in the cold eastern european winters, wine would be left out in the cold, and the water and other substances in the wine would rise to the top and freeze, where it could be scraped off, leaving a more pure drink behind. Though this process would make it more of a crude brandy. Later on, vodka was also commonly used for medicinal purposes, but it also became a more and more populat drink. Early production methods left many impurities in the drink, and distillers would mask the taste with fruit, spices and herbs. Then in the mid-15th century, pot distillation was introduced, and vodka began to ramp up in production, and in 1716, owning distilleries became exclusive to the rich. At the time, with these far and wide distilleries, the flavor of vodka wasn't standardized, with all different sorts of vodka and flavors. And in the 18th century, a professor came up with a way to purify alchohol with charcoal filtration. Finally, near the end of the 19th century, the tastes and production techniques of vodka were more standardized. After the russian revolution, many vodka producers emigrated to the rest of europe, where it was spread further to america. In the 1940s the drink achieved wider popularity.

The screwdriver was one of the first recorded cocktails made with vodka, and similar to other early drinks, wasn't made in a bar.There are many potential stories for how the drink came about. One says it was made by American oil workers in the Persian gulf in the middle of the 20th century. While working, they wanted a drink. So, they added some Vodka to their orange juice, and stir it with a screwdriver as that's what they had on hand. Another possibility is that it was made by American marines during WW2, while other stories say it was invented by Turkish intelligence agents, Balkan refugees and American engineers in the Park Hotel. However it came to be, it's now enjoyed as one of the easiest go-to drinks there is.
