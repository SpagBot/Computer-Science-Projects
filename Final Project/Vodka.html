The History of Whisky:In the 11th century, Celtic monks brought the art of distillation to Ireland, and they really only made wine. But on this island, it simply wasn't possible to grow. They had to be content with beer made from fermented barley, and by 1494, Whisky was first mentioned in a Scottish document. The first documented distillery was Bushmills in Ireland, in 1608. When the French wine supply was halved by pests in the late 18th century, the Scots were cut off from buying any. But one Scotsman had an idea: Why not store Whisky in sherry casks like cognac? The new concept was a major success, and led Whisky to the rest of the world. Today, it's well known as one of the most popular liquors to both mix and enjoy on it's own all around the world, being used in many of the best drinks ever made, some of which are listed below.What's the difference between Whiskey and Whisky? There are a couple reasons why the different spellings came to be, but today, it's just where the drink is from. In the USA and Ireland, it's Whiskey. In Scotland and Canada, it's Whisky. Throughout this page, they're used often interchangeably.

The screwdriver was one of the first recorded cocktails made with vodka, and similar to other early drinks, wasn't made in a bar.There are many potential stories for how the drink came about. One says it was made by American oil workers in the Persian gulf in the middle of the 20th century. While working, they wanted a drink. So, they added some Vodka to their orange juice, and stir it with a screwdriver as that's what they had on hand. Another possibility is that it was made by American marines during WW2, while other stories say it was invented by Turkish intelligence agents, Balkan refugees and American engineers in the Park Hotel. However it came to be, it's now enjoyed as one of the easiest go-to drinks there is.

The old fashioned is one of the earliest drinks ever conceived, and to this day is the most popular drink ever. When mixed drinks were first being made, the word cocktail really only meant a spirit, bitters and sugar, and that's what this is. It's spawned many variants, being such a simple starting point, and even the garnish is disputed. It's often garnished with just an orange twist, but I prefer rabbits ears, which is both a lemon and orange slice. Some people smoke the glass, some light the twists, and of course everybody has their whiskey preference, but most old fashioneds are made with some type of bourbon.
