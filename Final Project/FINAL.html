Syrups

Unsurprisingly, the most simple syrup. To make, just put a 1:1 mixture of sugar and water over heat until the sugar dissolves. That's it.

One step above simple syrup, rich simple syrup strays away from a 1:1 mixture and instead adds more sugar. Not only does this make the drink sweeter, but it also gives the syrup a slight orange hue which can be utlizied in certain situations. It often uses a 3:2 mixture but it can vary by preference.

Why James Bond Sucks

Many of us are familiar with James Bond's drink of choice- a vodka martini. Shaken, not stirred. It's a signature line, and it doesn't make a lot of sense. there's an important difference between shaken and stirred drinks. When a drink is shaken, it incorporates fluids of different consistency well and sometimes adds foam, and often gets the drink colder and more diluted. Stirred drinks are for more alchohol-forward drinks, like an old fashioned, with simple ingredients that don't need to be diluted as much. Like a martini. Why would you shake a vodka martini? It just dilutes it more.
